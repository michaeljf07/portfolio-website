The best sales pitches usually don’t happen behind a mahogany podium with a polished 20-slide deck. They happen in the heat of the moment, fueled by caffeine, adrenaline, and a product you’ve spent enough late nights building that you know every pixel by heart.

Last week, I attended [Meraki UofT (Season 5, Episode 1)](https://www.merakiuoft.com/). I walked into the room expecting to be a fly on the wall—to listen, learn, and maybe swap a few LinkedIn profiles. Instead, I ended up standing in front of 40 people with five minutes of prep time and a live demo that could have crashed at any second.

### The Power of Showing Up

Meraki has a reputation for bringing together the most ambitious builders in Toronto, and this session was no exception. Before the "official" part of the evening even started, I had already met over a dozen founders.

In a world where we spend so much time networking behind screens, there is an irreplaceable energy in a room of 40 people who are all trying to build something from nothing. I was content just being part of that energy until an organizer pulled me aside and asked the question every founder both loves and fears:

> _"Hey, do you want to pitch?"_

### The Pitch: What is Cache?

With no slides to lean on, I had to rely entirely on the vision. I stood up to present [Cache](https://cacheinyourcloset.com).

At its core, Cache is an AI-powered fashion resale marketplace. But more specifically, it’s a solution for the modern creator economy. We help influencers sell their 1-of-1 used clothing directly to the people who value it most: their followers.

I walked the room through the website and the app—both of which we’ve built from scratch over the last few months. I talked about the friction in current resale platforms and how Cache uses AI to bridge the gap between a creator’s closet and a fan’s wardrobe.

### The Fumble and the Recovery

If you’ve ever done a live demo, you know the "Demo Gods" are rarely kind.

Halfway through the presentation, I hit a snag. I almost fumbled the flow. In a slide-based presentation, a mistake feels like a failure; in a live demo, it’s just reality. I took a breath, cracked a joke, and recovered.

Actually, the "fumble" ended up being the best part. It showed the room that the product was real, the code was live, and the person behind it was human. The crowd was incredibly supportive, and the Q&A session afterward proved that the problem we’re solving with Cache resonates far beyond just the fashion world.

### What’s Next for Cache?

This pitch was a snapshot of where we are today, but the "glow-up" is already in progress. We are currently working on:

- **A complete UI/UX redesign** to make the shopping experience even more seamless.
- **New AI features** to help creators list items faster than ever.
- **Backend optimizations** to handle our growing community.

### The Lesson: Community over Everything

The biggest takeaway from my night at Meraki wasn't just the practice pitching; it was the realization of what a founder community is supposed to feel like. It’s a place where a surprise pitch lands instead of flops because everyone in the room knows how hard it is to stand up there.

Toronto’s tech scene is alive and well, and if you’re building something, my advice is simple: **Show up. Bring a live link. Be ready to pitch on five minutes' notice.**
